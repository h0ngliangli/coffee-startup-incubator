# Coffee Startup Club - Week 9: Mini Trial Sale

This is not a practice run. This is the real thing — just smaller.

Today you will sell coffee to real customers (teachers, classmates, or school staff) during a break. Real money. Real feedback. Real pressure.

The point of a trial sale is not to make as much money as possible. It is to find out what breaks when you put everything together — before the big sales event. Every problem you discover today is a problem you won't have then.

## Before You Open

Run through this checklist before your first customer arrives:

**Workspace**
- [ ] Table wiped down and clean
- [ ] All equipment rinsed and ready
- [ ] Supplies organized (cups, lids, stir sticks, napkins)
- [ ] Trash bag accessible

**Food Safety**
- [ ] Hands washed
- [ ] Milk and cold ingredients kept cold
- [ ] No expired ingredients

**Operations**
- [ ] Menu clearly displayed
- [ ] Prices visible
- [ ] Cash box ready with change (small bills and coins)
- [ ] Order tracking sheet ready (tally what you sell)

**Team roles assigned:**
- Who takes orders?
- Who handles payment?
- Who makes the drinks?
- Who manages the queue if it gets long?

## During the Sale

**Stay calm.** Things will go wrong. A cup will spill. Someone will ask for something you don't have. You'll run low on an ingredient. These are normal. Stay calm and work through them.

**Track everything.** Every drink sold gets a tally mark. You need this data for the debrief.

**Collect feedback.** After handing a drink to a customer, ask one question: *"How is it?"* Or put out a simple 3-question feedback card:
1. How was the drink? (1–5 stars)
2. Was the service friendly and fast? (Yes / No)
3. Would you buy from us again? (Yes / No)

**Observe what's hard.** Notice where you slow down. Notice what causes confusion. That's your improvement list.

## After the Sale: Debrief

Once the sale is over, sit down as a team before the session ends. Answer these questions honestly:

**Operations:**
- What slowed you down the most?
- Did you run out of anything?
- Were the roles clear? Did everyone know what to do?

**Product:**
- Which drink sold most?
- Did customers ask for anything you didn't have?
- Was there anything customers didn't like about a drink?

**Customer service:**
- Were there any uncomfortable moments with customers?
- What do customers seem to respond to most positively?

**Finances:**
- How many drinks did you sell?
- What was your total revenue?
- Estimate your COGS. What was your gross profit?
- Did you hit your break-even number?

## What to Improve Before the Real Event

Based on your debrief, each team should write down:
1. The single biggest operational problem to fix
2. One product change or addition
3. One thing to do better in customer service

These three things become your action items for Week 10 and the final event.

---

## Reflection

Regardless of how many drinks you sold or how smoothly things went — you did something most adults never do. You put yourself out there, made something, and sold it to real people.

Write down in your notebook: *What surprised you most about today?*

That answer often points to where your biggest growth is.
