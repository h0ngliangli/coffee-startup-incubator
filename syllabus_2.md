## Program Overview

The Coffee Startup Club is a project-based extracurricular program that guides middle school students through the full lifecycle of building and operating a real small business. Using coffee as the product medium — with an emphasis on age-appropriate cold and iced beverages — students develop practical skills in sourcing, operations, marketing, financial management, and sales.

The program is organized into three progressive phases: **Foundation**, **Operations**, and **Launch**. Each phase builds on the last, culminating in a student-run pop-up business that serves the school or local community.

---

## Program Goals

By the end of the program, students will be able to:

- Understand the key components of starting and running a small business
- Apply basic financial literacy skills including budgeting, pricing, and profit tracking
- Design and execute a simple marketing campaign using digital and in-person channels
- Safely and responsibly prepare and serve cold coffee and non-coffee beverages
- Collaborate as a team to solve real operational challenges
- Communicate their business story through a pitch presentation

## Safety & Compliance

Student safety is the top priority. The following policies apply at all times:

- **No hot beverages** will be prepared or served by students; all products are cold or iced
- All food handling follows **California Retail Food Code (CalCode)** guidelines
- Students handling food products must complete a brief **food safety orientation** at program start
- A faculty advisor or designated adult supervisor must be present during all product preparation and sales sessions
- **Allergen awareness** training is included in the Foundation phase
- Any on-campus sales require advance approval under the school's **Wellness Policy** and student activity regulations
- All off-campus events require signed **parent/guardian permission** and school administrator approval

## Detailed Weekly Schedule

### Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–6)

**Theme:** *Building the knowledge base before building the business.*

| Week | Topic | Key Activities | Deliverable |
|------|-------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | Welcome & Orientation | Club overview, team formation, intro to entrepreneurship vocabulary | Personal "Why I'm Here" statement |
| 2 | What Is a Business? | Business model canvas intro, case studies of student-run businesses | Draft Business Model Canvas |
| 3 | Coffee 101 | Origin, processing, cold brew vs. iced coffee vs. non-coffee options; supplier guest speaker | Product tasting notes worksheet |
| 4 | Know Your Customer | Customer personas, basic market research methods, school survey design | Customer survey (deployed) |
| 5 | Money Basics | Revenue, costs, profit; intro to budgeting; reading a simple P&L | Mock budget exercise |
| 6 | Food Safety & Legal Basics | California food handler basics, allergen labeling, school wellness policy, permits overview | Food safety quiz (pass required) |

---

### Phase 2 — Operations (Weeks 7–14)

**Theme:** *Turning ideas into a real, testable business.*

| Week | Topic | Key Activities | Deliverable |
|------|-------|---------------|-------------|
| 7 | Product Development | Recipe ideation, cold brew ratios, test batches; evaluate survey results | 3 tested product concepts |
| 8 | Sourcing & Suppliers | Supplier comparison, local partnerships, sustainability considerations | Supplier comparison matrix |
| 9 | Branding & Identity | Name, logo, brand voice; Canva workshop | Brand style tile |
| 10 | Pricing Strategy | Cost-plus vs. value-based pricing; breakeven calculations | Pricing sheet with margin analysis |
| 11 | Social Media Marketing | Platform selection for school audience, content calendar, photography basics | 1-week content calendar |
| 12 | Sales & Customer Service | Sales techniques, handling objections, service scripts; role-play | Sales script + roleplay eval |
| 13 | Operations Simulation | Full dry run of service setup, order flow, cash handling | Ops checklist + debrief notes |
| 14 | Review & Refinement | Peer feedback on products, pricing, and branding; iterate on weak spots | Updated business plan draft |

---

### Phase 3 — Launch (Weeks 15–20)

**Theme:** *Run the business. Reflect. Pitch. Pass it on.*

| Week | Topic | Key Activities | Deliverable |
|------|-------|---------------|-------------|
| 15 | Pre-Launch Prep | Final product decisions, supply ordering, station setup, safety walkthrough | Launch readiness checklist |
| 16 | **Pop-Up Event #1** | First live sales session (school event or lunch period) | Sales log + daily P&L |
| 17 | Post-Event Debrief | What worked, what didn't; customer feedback review | Written reflection |
| 18 | **Pop-Up Event #2** | Second live sales session with improvements applied | Sales log + cumulative P&L |
| 19 | Pitch Preparation | Craft a 5-minute investor-style pitch; storytelling, data, and vision | Pitch deck (5–7 slides) |
| 20 | **Demo Day** | Student pitch presentations to panel (admin, parents, community mentors); profit distribution discussion | Final pitch + program portfolio |

## Grading & Assessment

This is an extracurricular program — **no academic grades are issued.** However, student progress is tracked through the following assessments, which are used for feedback and program improvement:

| Assessment | Description | When |
|------------|-------------|------|
| Food Safety Quiz | Must pass (≥80%) to participate in product sessions | Week 6 |
| Business Plan Draft | Evaluated by advisor using provided rubric | Week 14 |
| Peer Reviews | Structured feedback exchanges between teams | Weeks 10, 14 |
| Sales Performance Log | Accuracy and consistency of financial tracking | Weeks 16–18 |
| Final Pitch | Evaluated by panel on clarity, financials, and creativity | Week 20 |
| Program Portfolio | Collected work from all phases | Week 20 |

Students who complete all three phases and the final pitch will receive a **Certificate of Completion** and a letter of recognition suitable for college applications and club resumes.

## Succession & Club Continuity

To ensure the club survives beyond any single cohort:

## Appendix A — Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Business Model Canvas | A one-page visual framework describing how a business creates and delivers value |
| Cold Brew | Coffee brewed with cold water over 12–24 hours; lower acidity, no heat required |
| Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) | Direct costs of producing a product (ingredients, packaging) |
| Gross Margin | Revenue minus COGS, expressed as a percentage |
| Breakeven Point | The sales volume at which total revenue equals total costs |
| Customer Persona | A semi-fictional profile of an ideal customer |
| P&L Statement | Profit and Loss statement; summary of revenues and expenses |
| Pitch | A concise, persuasive presentation of a business idea to investors or stakeholders |
