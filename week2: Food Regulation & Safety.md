# Coffee Startup Club - Week 2: Food Regulation & Safety

Last week we talked about entrepreneurship and why we are doing this. This week, before we touch any equipment or make any drinks, we need to talk about something that every food business must get right: food safety.

This is not just a rule we follow because we have to. It's something we do because we care about our customers. A single food safety mistake can make someone sick — and that can end a business overnight. More importantly, it can hurt someone you know.

## Why Food Safety Matters for a Business

Think about this: you spend weeks building your brand, designing your menu, and practicing your drinks. Your first sale goes well. Then one customer gets a stomach ache. Word spreads. Would you come back? Would your friends?

Food safety is not just about health — it's about trust. And trust is the foundation of any business.

There are also legal requirements. In most places, anyone selling food to the public must follow food handling regulations set by local health authorities. Even student-run stands at school events fall under basic hygiene standards. Knowing these rules protects you and your customers.

## The Big Three of Food Safety

### 1. Clean
- Wash your hands thoroughly before handling any food or equipment — at least 20 seconds with soap
- Clean all surfaces and equipment before and after use
- Use separate cloths for wiping surfaces vs. handling food
- Keep your workspace free of clutter

### 2. Separate
- Keep raw ingredients away from ready-to-serve items
- Use separate containers for different ingredients
- Never reuse cups, straws, or utensils without washing them

### 3. Control Temperature
- Hot drinks must be served hot — above 140°F (60°C)
- Cold drinks must stay cold — below 40°F (4°C)
- Never leave milk or cream unrefrigerated for more than 2 hours
- Use a thermometer when in doubt

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Touching your face, hair, or phone and then handling food without washing hands
- Using the same spoon to stir and then taste without washing it first
- Leaving lids off containers
- Ignoring expiration dates on ingredients
- Letting someone who is feeling sick handle food

## Setting Up a Clean Workspace

A clean workspace is a prepared workspace. Before every session and every sales event, run through this checklist:

- [ ] Hands washed
- [ ] Surface wiped down with a clean cloth
- [ ] Equipment rinsed and ready
- [ ] Ingredients checked (no expired items)
- [ ] Trash bag available and accessible
- [ ] Paper towels or clean cloths on hand

## Part 2: Hands-On Practice

In this session, we will set up our workspace from scratch as if we are about to open for business.

**Activity:**
1. Each team sets up their station following the checklist above
2. Practice the full hand-washing routine — time it together (20 seconds minimum)
3. Instructor will do a "health inspector" walkthrough — identify anything that would fail a real inspection
4. Teams correct any issues found and re-pass the inspection

The goal: by the end of today, setting up a clean workspace should feel automatic.
