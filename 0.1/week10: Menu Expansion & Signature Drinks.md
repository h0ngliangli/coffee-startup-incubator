# Coffee Startup Club - Week 10: Menu Expansion & Signature Drinks

You know how to make coffee. You know how to sell it. You know what your customers think.

Now it's time to make something that is entirely yours.

## Beyond the Basics

The three foundation drinks we learned in Week 3 — black coffee, iced coffee, and milk coffee — are a starting point, not a ceiling. Every great coffee shop builds on these basics to create drinks that people travel across town for.

What makes a drink memorable?

- **A unique flavor combination** — something unexpected that works together
- **A great name** — the name is part of the experience
- **A story** — where did the idea come from? Why this combination?
- **Repeatability** — you can make it the same way every single time

## The Elements of a New Drink

When inventing a new drink, think about these variables:

**Base**
- Espresso-style strong coffee, pour-over, cold brew, decaf, tea

**Temperature**
- Hot, iced, blended/frozen

**Milk or no milk**
- Dairy, oat, almond, coconut, none

**Sweetener**
- None, simple syrup, honey, brown sugar, flavored syrup (vanilla, caramel, hazelnut)

**Add-ins**
- Spices (cinnamon, cardamom, nutmeg), extracts (vanilla, almond), toppings (whipped cream, sea salt, cocoa powder)

Mix and match. The best drinks often come from unexpected combinations. Some combinations will taste terrible — that's fine. That's how you find the ones that are great.

## What Makes a Good Signature Drink

A signature drink does three things:
1. **Tastes good** — this is non-negotiable
2. **Stands out** — it's different from what people can get anywhere else
3. **Fits your brand** — it feels like *you*. A stand called "Morning Crew" with a warm, cozy brand should have a drink that feels warm and cozy.

A signature drink can also be a conversation starter. When a customer asks "What's in this?", that's a marketing moment. Have a short, interesting answer ready.

## Pricing Your New Drink

Before you finalize any new drink, calculate its COGS using the method from Week 5. Add up every ingredient, packaging cost, and set a price that maintains your target margin.

If an ingredient is too expensive and pushes your COGS too high, find a substitute or simplify the recipe. Creativity has to work within the economics.

## Part 2: Drink Invention Session

Today each team will develop at least one original recipe to add to their menu for the sales event.

**The rules:**
- You must be able to make it consistently (same way every time)
- You must know the exact COGS before presenting
- You must have a name and a one-line description for the menu
- It must be something you are proud to serve

**Step 1 — Brainstorm (10 min)**
Each person on the team proposes one drink idea. Use the variables above as a framework. No idea is too weird at this stage.

**Step 2 — Narrow down (5 min)**
Vote on 1–2 ideas to actually make and test today.

**Step 3 — Make and taste (15 min)**
Brew your experimental drinks. Taste them. Adjust. Taste again.

**Step 4 — Calculate COGS (5 min)**
List every ingredient and its cost. Set your price.

**Step 5 — Name it and present it (10 min)**
Give your drink a name and a one-line menu description. Present it to the group. Everyone tastes each team's drink and gives one piece of feedback.
