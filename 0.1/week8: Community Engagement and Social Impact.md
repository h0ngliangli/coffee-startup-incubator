# Coffee Startup Club - Week 8: Community Engagement and Social Impact

So far we have built a product, a brand, a pricing model, and a customer service approach. This week we step back and ask a bigger question:

**Why does your business exist — beyond making money?**

Every lasting business has an answer to this question. And for a student-run business embedded in a school community, the answer can be genuinely powerful.

## What is Community Engagement?

Community engagement means actively participating in and contributing to the community around you — not just selling to it.

The difference:
- A business that **sells** to its community shows up when it wants money
- A business that **engages** its community shows up even when it's not selling

For your coffee stand, this could look like:
- Showing up at school events to support (not just to sell)
- Donating a portion of proceeds to a school club, cause, or project
- Sharing the story of your business with the school — your wins, your struggles, your lessons
- Recognizing loyal customers or giving back to people who supported you early

## Why Does It Matter for Business?

Community engagement is not just a feel-good addition. It has direct business benefits:

**Loyalty.** People support businesses they feel connected to. When customers feel like they are part of your story, they come back and bring others.

**Word of mouth.** The best marketing is someone telling their friend "You have to check out this coffee stand — these students are amazing." You can't buy that. You have to earn it.

**Trust.** A business that gives back signals that it cares about more than profit. That builds trust, which makes customers more forgiving when something goes wrong.

## Social Impact: What Will You Stand For?

This doesn't have to be complicated. Pick something your team genuinely cares about and connect your business to it.

Some ideas:
- Donate 10% of profits to a school program that needs funding
- Use eco-friendly cups and packaging and make that part of your brand story
- Feature a "student of the month" at your stand — highlight someone from your school who is doing something great
- Partner with a school club and sell at their events in exchange for help promoting yours

The key is **authenticity.** Pick something you actually care about, not something that sounds impressive. Customers — especially young ones — can tell the difference.

## Your Story is Your Impact

You are middle school students running a real business. That *is* the story. Not many people do this. Not many people even try.

When you sell coffee, you are not just selling a drink. You are showing your school community that young people can be competent, responsible, and entrepreneurial. That is a form of social impact in itself.

Lean into it. Tell that story on social media, in your booth signage, in how you talk to customers.

## Part 2: Community Engagement Planning

Today each team will create a community engagement plan for their sales event.

**Step 1 — Identify your community (5 min)**
Who is your community? Students in your grade? The whole school? Teachers? Parents? Be specific.

**Step 2 — Choose your cause (10 min)**
What will you stand for beyond selling coffee? Brainstorm as a team and agree on one thing. It should be something you all genuinely care about.

**Step 3 — Plan your engagement (15 min)**
Answer these questions:
- How will you tell your story at the event? (Signage, conversation, social media?)
- What will you do with a portion of your proceeds? (Even $5 donated means something.)
- How will you involve your community — not just sell to them?

**Step 4 — Present your plan (5 min per team)**
Share your engagement plan with the group.
