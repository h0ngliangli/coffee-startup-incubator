# What is the Coffee Startup Incubator?
The Coffee Startup Incubator is a program designed to teach middle school students the fundamentals of entrepreneurship through the lens of starting a mini coffee business. Over the course of 10 weeks, students will learn about the coffee industry, develop their brewing skills, create a brand, and ultimately sell their products to their school community. The program is structured around the design thinking process, encouraging students to empathize with their customers, define their needs, ideate creative solutions, prototype their products, and test them in the real world.
# Why Coffee?
Coffee is an ideal vehicle for teaching entrepreneurship because it is tangible, low-cost to start, and produces fast feedback. Students can see, taste, and refine their product within the same session — making abstract business concepts like product quality, customer preference, and pricing immediately concrete.

Unlike many school projects, a coffee stand is a real business: students must manage inventory, handle money, and satisfy actual customers. This creates genuine stakes and genuine learning.

To ensure the program is appropriate for all students, we will offer decaffeinated and non-coffee options (such as tea or hot chocolate) alongside regular coffee. No student will be required to consume caffeine as part of the program.

# Why Middle School Students?
Middle school is a critical time for developing skills and interests that can shape a student’s future. By introducing entrepreneurship at this age, we can inspire creativity, build confidence, and teach valuable life skills that will serve students well in high school and beyond. Plus, it’s a great way to engage students in a hands-on project that combines learning with real-world application.

# Program Overview
The Coffee Startup Incubator is a 10-week extracurricular program that teaches students the fundamentals of entrepreneurship through running a real coffee business. Students progress through five phases — Foundation, Craft, Brand, Sales, and Growth — covering topics from brewing techniques to financial literacy. Each weekly session combines a short lecture or discussion with a hands-on activity, grounded in the design thinking process: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, and test. The program culminates in a real sales event where students apply everything they have learned.

# Weekly Schedule
Each weekly session will be split into two parts: a lecture or discussion component and a hands-on activity. Here’s a brief overview of what we will cover each week:
Week 1: 
- part 1: Introduction to Entrepreneurship and the Coffee Industry
- part 2: Hands-on coffee beans tasting and brewing basics (using a simple pour-over method)
Week 2: 
- part 1: Food Regulation & Safety — Learn the basics of food hygiene and safe food handling before any hands-on work
- part 2: Hands-on practice with food safety protocols and setting up a clean workspace
Week 3: 
- part 1: Coffee Brewing Hands-On — Learn basic brewing techniques and experiment with different recipes to find your signature drink
- part 2: Hands-on practice with brewing techniques and recipe experimentation
Week 4: 
- part 1: Marketing
- part 2: Hands-on branding, marketing and booth design activities
Week 5: 
- part 1: Product Development and Pricing — Including a basic introduction to cost and profit concepts
- part 2: Hands-on product development and pricing activities
Week 6: 
- part 1: Sales and Customer Service — Role-play selling scenarios in class to practice before the real thing
- part 2: Hands-on sales and customer service practice
Week 7: 
- part 1: Financial Literacy and Business Management — Apply pricing and profit concepts from Week 5 to our own business plan
- part 2: Hands-on financial literacy and business management activities
Week 8: 
- part 1: Community Engagement and Social Impact
- part 2: Hands-on community engagement activities
Week 9: 
- part 1: Mini Trial Sale — Sell to teachers or classmates during a break; gather real customer feedback
- part 2: Hands-on trial sale activities
Week 10: Menu Expansion & Signature Drinks
- part 1: The basics we covered are just the starting point — learn how to invent new drinks, experiment with flavors, and build a menu that reflects your team's identity
- part 2: Hands-on drink invention session: each team develops and presents at least one original recipe to add to their menu for the sales event

# Community Engagement
Community engagement is a core part of the program. Students will set up booths at school events to sell their products at reasonable prices and interact with real customers. This experience builds empathy, communication skills, and financial responsibility. A mid-program mini trial sale (Week 9) gives students a low-stakes opportunity to practice before the final event.

# Curriculum Alignment
The program directly supports several areas of the school's academic and social-emotional learning goals:
- **Math:** Pricing, cost calculation, profit and loss, and basic budgeting
- **Language Arts:** Written and verbal communication, pitching ideas, customer interaction
- **Social Studies:** Understanding local and global economies, community impact
- **SEL:** Teamwork, resilience, growth mindset, and self-directed learning

# Budget and Resources
The program is designed to be low-cost and self-sustaining. Estimated startup costs cover basic equipment (a coffee maker, a kettle, and serving supplies) and initial inventory. Once the program is underway, sales revenue will offset ongoing supply costs. A detailed budget proposal is available upon request.

# Supervision and Safety
All sessions will be supervised by the program instructor. Students handling hot equipment will receive a brief safety orientation before any hands-on activity. Food handling guidelines will follow the school's existing protocols for student-run food activities. All off-campus sales events will require appropriate permission slips and chaperone coverage.

# How We Will Measure Success
At the end of the program, we will evaluate outcomes across three areas:
- **Student learning:** Pre- and post-program self-assessments on entrepreneurship and financial literacy concepts
- **Business performance:** Revenue generated, customer satisfaction feedback from the sales event
- **Engagement:** Attendance, participation, and student reflections on what they learned
