# Coffee Startup Club - Week 3: Coffee Brewing Hands-On

You passed the food safety inspection. Now we get to make coffee.

This week is about building your craft. Every great coffee business starts with a great product. Before you worry about your brand, your pricing, or your Instagram page — you need to be able to make a drink that people will pay for and come back for.

## What Makes a Good Cup of Coffee?

Ask ten people what makes a good cup of coffee and you'll get ten different answers. That's actually important — it means there is no single right answer, and your job as a coffee maker is to understand what *your* customers want.

That said, there are fundamentals that make a consistently good cup:

- **The right ratio** — Too much water makes it weak. Too little makes it bitter. A standard starting ratio is 1:15 (1 gram of coffee to 15 grams of water).
- **Fresh ingredients** — Coffee goes stale. Always use fresh beans or grounds. Check the roast date when possible.
- **Water temperature** — Water that is too hot burns the coffee. Too cool and it under-extracts. The sweet spot is around 195–205°F (90–96°C) — just off the boil.
- **Consistency** — Whatever you do, do it the same way every time. Customers expect the same drink each visit.

## Our Core Menu (Starting Point)

Today we will learn to make our three foundation drinks. These are not the final menu — they are the base. By Week 10, you will have invented your own on top of these.

**Drink 1: Classic Black Coffee (Pour-Over)**
- What you need: coffee grounds, hot water, filter, pour-over dripper, cup
- Process: Bloom the grounds with a small amount of hot water for 30 seconds, then pour slowly in circles

**Drink 2: Iced Coffee**
- Same as above, brewed strong, poured over ice
- Ratio adjustment: brew at 1:10 (stronger) to account for ice dilution

**Drink 3: Milk-Based Coffee (Hot or Iced)**
- Add steamed or cold milk to black coffee
- Ratio: approximately 1 part coffee to 2 parts milk — adjust to taste
- Decaf and non-dairy milk options available for all customers

> **Note:** For students who prefer not to consume caffeine, all three drinks can be made with decaf grounds using the exact same technique.

## Taste Like a Pro

Professional coffee tasters use a method called **cupping** to evaluate coffee. We will borrow a simplified version:

When you taste your drink, think about:
- **Aroma** — What does it smell like before you drink it?
- **Acidity** — Does it have a brightness or sharpness?
- **Body** — Is it light and watery, or thick and full?
- **Sweetness** — Natural sweetness, not added sugar
- **Finish** — What does it taste like after you swallow?

You don't need to use these exact words. Just train your palate to notice more than "good" or "bad."

## Part 2: Hands-On Brewing

Each team will brew all three foundation drinks today.

**Instructions:**
1. Set up your clean workspace first (from Week 2 checklist)
2. Brew each drink following the method above
3. Taste your own drinks and rate them using the tasting framework
4. Swap drinks with another team — taste theirs and give feedback
5. Identify one thing you would change in your next brew

**What we're looking for:** Not perfection. Consistency and awareness. Can you make the same drink twice and have it taste the same?
