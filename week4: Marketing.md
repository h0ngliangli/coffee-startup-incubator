# Coffee Startup Club - Week 4: Marketing

You can make a great cup of coffee. But if nobody knows about you, nobody buys from you.

This week is about getting the word out — and more than that, making people *want* to seek you out. That's what marketing is. Not just advertising. Not just a logo. It's the whole feeling people get when they encounter your business.

## What is a Brand?

A brand is not a logo. A logo is just a symbol.

A brand is what people *think and feel* when they see your name. Think about the difference between McDonald's and a five-star restaurant. Both sell food. But they make you feel completely different things — and that difference is entirely in the branding.

Your brand answers three questions:
1. **Who are you?** — Your name, your personality, your values
2. **Who are you for?** — Your target customer
3. **Why should they choose you?** — Your unique value

For our coffee stand, we have a natural advantage: we are students. That is our story. People want to support young entrepreneurs. Lead with that.

## The Elements of a Brand

**Name**
Your name should be memorable, easy to say, and hint at what you do or who you are. Avoid generic names like "Good Coffee" — they don't stick. Think about names that tell a small story.

> Examples: "Morning Crew," "The Grind Club," "Brew Bunch," "First Sip"

**Tagline**
A short phrase that captures your promise. One sentence, max.
> Examples: "Made by students. Loved by everyone." / "Your day, better."

**Visual Identity**
- **Colors:** Choose 2–3 colors that match the feeling you want to create. Warm tones (brown, orange, cream) feel cozy. Bright tones feel energetic.
- **Fonts:** Use clean, readable fonts for a menu or poster. Decorative fonts can be used for your logo but keep them simple.
- **Logo:** It doesn't need to be complex. A simple icon + your name is enough.

**Voice**
How do you sound on a poster, on social media, when you talk to a customer? Friendly? Professional? Playful? Pick one and be consistent.

## Where to Market

**At school:**
- Posters in hallways and common areas
- Announcements during school events
- Word of mouth — tell your friends, tell their friends

**Social media:**
- Instagram and TikTok are your strongest tools
- Short videos of the brewing process perform well — people love watching drinks being made
- Consistency matters more than perfection: post regularly, even if it's just a photo of your setup

**At the event itself:**
- Your stand design is marketing — make it look good (see the Marketing module for stand design principles)
- A chalkboard menu is more inviting than a printed sheet
- Smile and make eye contact — you are the brand ambassador

## Part 2: Hands-On Branding Activity

Today each team will develop their brand identity from scratch.

**Step 1 — Name brainstorm (5 min)**
Each person writes down 3 name ideas. Share with the team. Vote and narrow to one.

**Step 2 — Visual identity sketch (15 min)**
On paper: sketch a simple logo, pick your 2–3 colors, choose a font style (block letters, handwritten, etc.)

**Step 3 — Tagline (5 min)**
Write 3 tagline options. Pick the one that feels most like your team.

**Step 4 — Stand design sketch (10 min)**
Quickly sketch what your booth will look like. Where does your name go? What does the menu board look like? What makes someone walking by stop and look?

**Step 5 — Pitch it (5 min per team)**
Present your brand to the group in 60 seconds: name, tagline, colors, and one sentence on why someone should buy from you.
