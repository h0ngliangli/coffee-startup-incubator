# Why Coffee?
Coffee is more than just a drink. It’s a global industry that touches the lives of millions of people every day. By starting a mini coffee business, students can learn about entrepreneurship, marketing, customer service, and more. Plus, it’s a fun and creative way to bring people together and make a positive impact in our community.

# Why Middle School Students?
Middle school is a critical time for developing skills and interests that can shape a student’s future. By introducing entrepreneurship at this age, we can inspire creativity, build confidence, and teach valuable life skills that will serve students well in high school and beyond. Plus, it’s a great way to engage students in a hands-on project that combines learning with real-world application.

# Program Overview
The Coffee Startup Incubator is a 10-week program designed to teach students the fundamentals of starting and running a coffee business. Each week, we will cover a different aspect of the business, from understanding the coffee industry to developing a brand and selling our products. The program will culminate in a real sales event where students will have the opportunity to apply what they’ve learned and see the results of their hard work.

# Put Design Thinking into Practice
Right at the heart of our program is the design thinking process. We will encourage students to empathize with their customers, define their needs, ideate creative solutions, prototype their products, and test them in the real world. This iterative process will help students develop a deep understanding of their customers and create products that truly meet their needs. It will also foster a mindset of experimentation and learning from failure, which is essential for any entrepreneur.

# Program Structure
The program is structured into five phases, each with its own focus and activities. We will start with the foundation of understanding what a business is and how coffee fits into it, then move on to developing our craft, building our brand, preparing for sales, and finally growing our business. Each phase will include a mix of lectures, discussions, hands-on activities, and guest speakers to provide a well-rounded learning experience.

# Community Engagement
Community engagement is a crucial part of the learning process. Throughout the program, students will set up booths at school events to sell their coffee with reasonable prices and interact with customers. This real-world experience will help them in many aspects of the business, from developing empathy by understanding customer needs to handling money. It will also give them a sense of accomplishment and pride in their work.
